The Williams Companies, Inc. v. Energy Transfer Equity, L.P. (coordinated with C.A. No. 12337-VCG)Centered Axis

Del. Ch. · C.A. Nos. 12168-VCG & 12337-VCG

After signing a Sep. 28, 2015 Merger Agreement worth tens of billions, energy markets collapsed and Energy Transfer Equity (ETE) soured on the deal. ETE's tax counsel Latham & Watkins concluded in April 2016 that it could no longer deliver the §721 tax opinion that was a closing condition. Williams sued in Chancery on two coordinated tracks: a First Action challenging ETE's defensive March 2016 'Special Issuance' of Series A Convertible Preferred Units, and a Second Action challenging Latham's inability to issue the 721 Opinion. After a two-day trial June 20–21, 2016, Vice Chancellor Glasscock ruled June 24, 2016 (the eve of the Outside Date) that Latham acted in good faith, ETE used commercially reasonable efforts, and ETE was contractually entitled to terminate the Merger Agreement on the failed closing condition.
