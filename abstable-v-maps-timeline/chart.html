AB Stable VIII LLC v. MAPS Hotels and Resorts One LLCDual Track — Del. · No. 71, 2021 (on appeal from C.A. No. 2020-0310, Del. Ch.)

AB Stable VIII LLC (Anbang-affiliated 'Seller') agreed to sell fifteen luxury U.S. hotels (the Strategic portfolio) to MAPS Hotels and Resorts One LLC (Mirae-affiliated 'Buyer') for $5.8 billion under a Sept. 10, 2019 Sale and Purchase Agreement. Closing was delayed by both fraudulent-deed problems on the underlying real estate and the COVID-19 pandemic. The Seller closed hotels, furloughed/laid off over 5,200 FTE employees, and made other drastic operational changes without Buyer consent. The Buyer terminated; the Seller sued in the Court of Chancery for specific performance. After an August 2020 expedited trial, the Court of Chancery (Laster, V.C., post-trial opinion Nov. 30, 2020) held the Seller breached the Ordinary Course Covenant and a title-insurance closing condition failed. The Delaware Supreme Court AFFIRMED on Dec. 8, 2021 (Seitz, C.J., en banc), resting on the Ordinary Course Covenant breach alone.
