Shearson/American Express Inc. v. McMahonCentered Axis

U.S. · No. 86-44

Customers (the McMahons) sued their brokerage (Shearson/American Express) and its representative for fraudulent excessive trading, alleging §10(b)/Rule 10b-5, RICO, and state-law claims. Brokers moved to compel arbitration under FAA §3, relying on a predispute arbitration clause in the customer agreements. The District Court compelled arbitration of the Exchange Act and state claims but kept RICO; the Second Circuit reversed as to §10(b) and affirmed as to RICO. The Supreme Court reversed and remanded, holding both the §10(b) and RICO claims arbitrable under the FAA.
