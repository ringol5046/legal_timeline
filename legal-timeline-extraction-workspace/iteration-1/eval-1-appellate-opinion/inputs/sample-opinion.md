## MEMORANDUM OPINION

**Beresford, J.** — January 17, 2025

This is an FLSA retaliation and wrongful termination action. Plaintiff Janet Wells alleges that her former employer, Drayton Holdings, Inc. ("Drayton"), terminated her employment in retaliation for raising complaints about unpaid overtime. Drayton has moved for summary judgment under Rule 56. For the reasons that follow, the Court **DENIES** the motion as to the FLSA retaliation claim and **GRANTS** the motion as to the breach of implied contract claim.

---

### I. BACKGROUND

#### A. Factual Background

Wells was hired by Drayton as a Senior Operations Analyst on **March 4, 2019**. Compl. ¶ 12; Wells Dep. 18:5-22. She reported directly to Operations Director Michael Chu. Wells Decl. ¶ 3 (Ex. P-1).

In **early 2022**, Wells began working extended hours to absorb the workload of two analysts who had departed. She estimates she worked an average of 53 hours per week from February through October 2022. Wells Decl. ¶¶ 8-11. Drayton's timekeeping records, however, show Wells clocking 40-44 hours per week during this period. Chu Decl. ¶ 14 (Ex. D-3). This discrepancy is contested, and the Court does not resolve it here.

On **September 8, 2022**, Wells sent an email to Chu raising concerns about uncompensated overtime, attaching a spreadsheet of her hours worked. Ex. P-4 (Wells/Chu email chain). The parties dispute when Drayton's HR department received this complaint. Wells contends that Chu forwarded the email to HR Director Elaine Morris that same day, **September 8, 2022**. Wells Dep. 42:18-43:7. Drayton contends — and Morris testified — that she did not receive any such forwarded message until **October 3, 2022**, when Chu first raised it in a meeting. Morris Dep. 31:4-19; Ex. D-7 (Morris's calendar). This factual dispute is material to the temporal proximity element of Wells's retaliation claim.

On **October 14, 2022**, Wells received a performance improvement plan ("PIP") citing alleged deficiencies in client communication. Ex. P-9. Wells testified that she had received "exceeds expectations" on her 2021 annual review and that the PIP was pretextual. Wells Dep. 67:8-21; Ex. P-2 (2021 review). Drayton points to two client complaints received in July and August 2022 as the basis for the PIP. Chu Decl. ¶¶ 22-25; Exs. D-9, D-10.

On **December 19, 2022**, Drayton terminated Wells's employment. The termination letter cites "failure to satisfy PIP milestones." Ex. P-12.

#### B. Procedural History

Wells filed her Complaint on **April 11, 2023**, asserting (1) FLSA retaliation under 29 U.S.C. § 215(a)(3); (2) wrongful termination in violation of public policy; and (3) breach of implied contract based on Drayton's progressive discipline policy. Dkt. 1.

Drayton filed an Answer on **May 22, 2023**. Dkt. 14. The Court entered a scheduling order on **June 30, 2023**, setting fact discovery to close on April 1, 2024. Dkt. 19.

The parties engaged in extensive discovery, including 12 depositions and production of approximately 8,400 pages of documents. On **March 27, 2024**, the parties stipulated to extend the discovery deadline to May 31, 2024. Dkt. 47.

Drayton filed the present Motion for Summary Judgment on **August 8, 2024**. Dkt. 62. Wells filed her opposition on **September 12, 2024**. Dkt. 71. Drayton filed its reply on **September 26, 2024**. Dkt. 75. The Court heard oral argument on **November 14, 2024**.

### II. STANDARD OF REVIEW

Summary judgment is appropriate "if the movant shows that there is no genuine dispute as to any material fact and the movant is entitled to judgment as a matter of law." Fed. R. Civ. P. 56(a). [Citations omitted.]

### III. DISCUSSION

#### A. FLSA Retaliation

[...] The temporal proximity between the protected activity and the adverse employment action is sharply contested in this case. If Wells's version controls — that HR received her overtime complaint on September 8, 2022 — the gap between protected activity and the October 14 PIP is approximately five weeks, which courts in this Circuit have found sufficient to support an inference of causation. *See* *Williams v. Phila. Hous. Auth.*, 380 F.3d 751, 760 (3d Cir. 2004). If Drayton's version controls — that HR first learned of the complaint on October 3, 2022 — the gap shrinks to eleven days, which is even more probative.

In either event, a reasonable jury could find temporal proximity sufficient. Summary judgment is **DENIED** as to the FLSA retaliation claim.

#### B. Breach of Implied Contract

[...] Wells has not established that Drayton's progressive discipline policy created an enforceable contract under Pennsylvania law. Summary judgment is **GRANTED** as to this claim.

### IV. CONCLUSION

For the foregoing reasons, Defendant's Motion for Summary Judgment is **DENIED IN PART and GRANTED IN PART**. The retaliation and wrongful termination claims will proceed to trial. The implied contract claim is dismissed.
