## ANSWER

Defendant Pelham Properties, LLC ("Pelham") answers Plaintiff Northstar Construction, Inc.'s ("Northstar") Complaint as follows:

1. Admits the allegations of Paragraphs 1-3 regarding the parties and Property.

2. **Admits** that the parties entered into the Construction Agreement on June 15, 2022 (¶ 4) and that Northstar mobilized on August 1, 2022 (¶ 6).

3. **Admits** that Change Orders No. 1 (November 4, 2022) and No. 2 (February 28, 2023) were issued (¶¶ 7-8).

4. **Denies** that Northstar substantially completed the work on October 22, 2023 (¶ 9). Pelham contends that **substantial completion was not achieved until at least January 30, 2024**, due to deficient seismic anchoring discovered during owner walkthroughs in November and December 2023.

5. Admits that Pelham timely paid progress draws through Draw #14 (¶ 10).

6. Admits that Draws #15 and #16 were submitted (¶¶ 11-12) but **denies** that Pelham's refusal to pay was wrongful, on the basis of defective work and unliquidated offsets.

7. Admits the recording of the Mechanic's Lien (¶ 13) but **denies** its validity.

8. **Denies** all remaining allegations not expressly admitted.

## AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSES

**First**: Failure of consideration / material breach (work was non-conforming).
**Second**: Offset (Pelham's damages exceed Northstar's claimed sums).
**Third**: Mechanic's Lien is invalid for noncompliance with Cal. Civ. Code § 8416.

## COUNTERCLAIM

Counterclaimant Pelham, against Counter-Defendant Northstar, alleges:

### General Allegations

15. The Construction Agreement, executed **June 15, 2022**, required Northstar to perform all work in a good and workmanlike manner consistent with the approved plans (Ex. A to Complaint, § 3.1).

16. Beginning in **March 2023**, Pelham raised concerns about deviations from the approved seismic anchoring specifications. **Ex. P-CC-1** (Pelham/Northstar correspondence). Pelham asserts these concerns were not adequately addressed.

17. On **June 12, 2023**, Pelham retained a third-party engineering firm, Wexford Structural Engineering ("Wexford"), to perform an independent inspection. Wexford issued a written report on **August 4, 2023** identifying twelve specific deviations from spec. **Ex. P-CC-2** (Wexford report).

18. Pelham forwarded the Wexford report to Northstar on **August 11, 2023**. **Ex. P-CC-3**. Northstar disputed the findings in a written response dated **August 25, 2023**. **Ex. P-CC-4**.

19. The parties met on **September 14, 2023** to attempt resolution. No agreement was reached. **Ex. P-CC-5** (meeting minutes).

20. On **November 6, 2023**, after Northstar's purported completion, Pelham conducted an owner walkthrough and identified continuing seismic anchoring deficiencies, as well as new defects in the rooftop deck waterproofing. **Ex. P-CC-6** (walkthrough notes).

21. On **December 4, 2023**, Pelham issued a formal Notice of Defective Work to Northstar pursuant to Section 12.3 of the Contract. **Ex. P-CC-7**. Northstar did not cure within the contractual 30-day period.

22. As of the date of this filing, the deficiencies remain uncured and have caused Pelham to incur remediation costs estimated at **$1,420,000**.

### First Cause of Action (Breach of Contract)

23. By failing to perform work in conformance with the approved plans and by refusing to cure after Notice of Defective Work, Northstar materially breached the Contract.
