# Timeline: Partnership Meeting re: Removal of J. Halverson

**Case:** *Halverson v. Smith Capital Partners*, No. 24-CV-00891 (S.D. Cal.)
**Purpose:** Motion in Limine — Contradiction between Smith deposition testimony and contemporaneous emails (Ex. 4, Ex. 7)

---

## Summary of the Contradiction

Gregory T. Smith testified under oath on October 22, 2024 that the partnership meeting at which Halverson's removal was decided occurred on **Friday, May 3, 2024**, that scheduling was handled **verbally / in person with no emails**, and that the removal notice was sent on **Monday, May 6, 2024**. The contemporaneous emails (Ex. 4 and Ex. 7) establish that the meeting in fact occurred on **Friday, May 10, 2024**, was scheduled **by email on May 7**, and that the removal notice was scheduled to be sent on **Monday, May 13, 2024**. Smith's testimony is off by exactly one week on every key date and is directly contradicted by emails he himself authored.

## Key Takeaways for Motion in Limine

1. **Date error is exactly 7 days** on every anchor (meeting: 5/3 vs. 5/10; notice: 5/6 vs. 5/13). This pattern suggests either witness confusion about week-of or deliberate misstatement, not innocent imprecision.
2. **Smith authored the contradicting email** (Ex. 4, 5/7/2024 4:33 PM). He cannot credibly claim lack of knowledge.
3. **Two independent witnesses (Anand, Wilkes) corroborate the email record** in real time on May 10.
4. **Smith's "no emails" testimony is independently false** apart from the date issue — Ex. 4 alone establishes scheduling occurred by email.
5. **The substantive meeting details** (two hours, three partners, unanimous, no notes) are consistent between deposition and emails — isolating the contradiction to dates and to the existence of scheduling emails.
