Wanke, Industrial, Commercial, Residential, Inc. v. KeckCentered Axis

Cal. Ct. App. 4th Dist., Div. 1 · D058669 / D058825 (Super. Ct. No. 37-2008-00097163-CU-BC-CTL)

Former employees Scott Keck and Robert Bozarth left Wanke (a commercial waterproofing contractor) in early 2008 to form WP Solutions, allegedly soliciting Wanke's customers. Wanke sued in December 2008. The parties settled in October 2009 with a five-year Stipulated Injunction barring solicitation of named Wanke customers. Wanke later moved to enforce the injunction on (i) Con Am Management contacts (May–Aug 2010 contempt trial + motion to enforce) and (ii) the Saratoga West / AV Builders project (Sept 2010). The trial court acquitted on contempt (finding the injunction invalid under Bus. & Prof. Code §16600 except for actually-confidential information) and denied the Saratoga West motion. The Court of Appeal (Aaron, J., Oct. 4, 2012) held that the double-jeopardy clause bars review of the contempt acquittal, but reversed on the motions-to-enforce: a facially valid stipulated injunction cannot be defended against on the ground that it is invalid. Petition for writ of mandate denied; appeals affirmed in part, reversed in part, and remanded.
